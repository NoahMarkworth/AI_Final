02 · SOLUTION

Hybrid Two-Layer Architecture

Fully auditable deterministic scoring + AI as a targeted, conditional escalation layer
INPUT
ESI level · vitals · chief complaint
age · imaging ordered · arrival mode
↓
Layer 1 — Deterministic Scoring
C1–C7 components produce a numerical urgency score. Every weight traces to a source document. Always runs.
Fully auditable No AI required
↓ only if tie ≤10 pts or incomplete data
Layer 2 — AI Reasoning (Conditional)
Activates only when Layer 1 cannot resolve — tiebreakers or missing data. Reasons about delay consequences at 20/60 min intervals.
GPT-4o Never the primary scorer
↓
OUTPUT
Ranked queue + plain-language rationale per patient + confidence flags

🔍 Core Design Principles

  • Every scoring weight cites a specific page and figure in the source documents
  • The deterministic layer makes the primary call — AI resolves ambiguity only

✓ What This Tool Is

A proof-of-concept advocacy tool — built to make the gap between current and possible future state concrete and quantifiable for Nebraska Medicine leadership.

✗ What This Tool Is Not

  • Not a clinical decision support system — does not diagnose
  • Does not replace physician judgment — surfaces information for humans

04 · CONDITIONS

54 Conditions — 6 Urgency Tiers

Each condition is mapped to a tier based on published clinical evidence about the consequence of imaging delay
TIER 1
+40 pts
Catastrophic — Death or irreversible harm measured in minutes
No safe waiting window. Every minute of imaging delay actively worsens outcome.
Tension pneumothorax · Ruptured AAA · Cardiac tamponade · STEMI+shock · Aortic dissection Type A · Massive hemothorax · Ruptured ectopic pregnancy
+40
TIER 2
+30 pts
Critical — Irreversible harm within 1–6 hours. Imaging delays eliminate treatment eligibility.
Established time-locked intervention windows — treatment doors close with each passing minute.
Ischemic stroke/LVO · ICH · Subarachnoid hemorrhage · Acute limb ischemia · Testicular/ovarian torsion · Cauda equina syndrome · Epidural hematoma · Boerhaave · Orbital compartment
+30
TIER 3
+20 pts
High — Irreversible harm window 6–24 hours. Delays measurably worsen outcome.
Brief queue wait acceptable with active monitoring — but compounding, not stagnant.
Mesenteric ischemia · Cholangitis Grade III · Subdural hematoma · Necrotizing fasciitis · Massive PE · HSV encephalitis · Fournier's gangrene · Ruptured spleen/liver
+20
TIER 4
+10 pts
Moderate-High — Clinical deterioration expected within 24–72 hours without imaging.
Meaningful but not catastrophic if imaging is briefly delayed.
Appendicitis · Bowel obstruction · NSTEMI/ACS · Spinal cord compression · Submassive PE · Carotid/vertebral dissection · Sigmoid volvulus
+10
TIER 5–6
+5 / 0
Moderate to Low — Brief delays unlikely to change ultimate outcome.
Imaging improves management but no significant time pressure absent deterioration.
Pneumonia characterization · Stable pancreatitis staging · Diverticulitis · Low-risk chest pain · Chronic condition follow-up · Incidental finding workup

05 · LAYER 2

AI Reasoning — Tiebreakers & Incomplete Data

Layer 2 activates only when Layer 1 cannot resolve with confidence — never as the primary scorer

⚡ Two Activation Triggers

  • Tiebreaker: Two patients score within 10 points — gap is below the model's reliable resolution. Also activates for the vital triad: SpO₂ <92% + HR >100 + RR >20.
  • Incomplete data: Missing ESI, missing vitals, or chief complaint too vague to map. Assigns a confidence flag and surfaces the gap for human review.

⏱ Delay Consequence Asymmetry

For each patient in a tiebreaker pair, the AI reasons about what specifically happens at +20 and +60 minutes of imaging delay — drawn directly from clinical time-sensitivity windows, not general knowledge.

Tiebreaker Walkthrough — AF-01 vs AF-04 (5-point gap)
AF-01 — Bowel Obstruction · Score 83 raw / 39 display (CT Abdomen ordered)
+20 min 71F with prior colostomy — adhesion-related obstruction. Bowel viability intact but closed-loop risk unconfirmed.
+60 min Closed-loop obstruction can progress to necrosis within hours in elderly patients with prior abdominal surgery.
AF-04 — Appendicitis · Score 78 raw / 37 display (CT Abdomen ordered)
+20 min No perforation signs. 33F with uncomplicated presentation. Guideline target is 3–6 hrs — still within window.

AI Decision: AF-01 goes first Both are Tier 4 abdominal emergencies only 5 raw points apart. Elderly post-surgical bowel obstruction carries higher closed-loop strangulation risk — consequence asymmetry tips the decision despite nearly identical scores.

Rationale Standards
✓ Clinically accurate All numbers and time windows drawn directly from source documents — no estimated values, no general knowledge substitutes.
✓ Plain-language accessible Legible to both clinical staff and non-clinical hospital leadership — the rationale is an advocacy document, not a physician note.
✓ Specific, not generic References this patient's specific condition, imaging type, and time window — never a boilerplate "this patient is sicker."
